Door Accessibility

Creating accessible accessories for Brandeis University

Bibliography

Problem Definition, Brainstorming, and Storyboarding

Problem Definition

Door knobs are a common fixture found in many of the dorms and buildings on Brandeis campus, however they can be difficult to use for people with wrist injuries. This project aims to create a solution that will make it easier for people with wrist injuries and mobility issues to open doors.

Thematic Analysis

Our thematic analysis resulted in 7 categories of ideas. These ideas allowed us to continue refining our final design to the following insight statements.

Insight Statements

  1. Door knobs are included in both residential and academic building design, but are not universal between buildings.
  2. People with wrist injuries or limited arm movement prefer pushing motions over twisting/pulling.
  3. People with access to 3D printers may or may not have access to the correct hardware to supplement our various design ideas.
  4. Many people appreciate door designs that avoid germ transmission/don’t require the use of hands to open.
  5. It is important to include access to a keyhole for door knobs with locking mechanisms.
  6. Interviewees expressed interest in a semi-permanently installed solution over a portable system.
  7. It is important for the doorknob to work on either side of a door, as well as on differently shaped door knobs.

How Might We?

  1. How might we adapt a design for multiple types of doorknobs?
  2. How might we combine the idea of a foot-operated door with an adapted door knob?
  3. How might we weigh accessibility of materials with durability and performance?
  4. How might we work with DCL to ensure our solution complies with their rules and safety regulations of the state?

Brainstorming

Final Idea

Our final idea is an attachment for a standard door knob that can be 3d printed and assembled with standard, off the shelf hardware to convert a door knob to a lever-style door handle. Our design will allow for easy installation and removable and will comply with the safety regulations of the state of Massachusetts.

Storyboarding

Interviews

Interviewee 1

Brandeis student from New Jersey who has injured their wrist in the past. Their injury came from past rock climbing and volleyball incidents that have resulted in lasting pain and mobility problems. Due to their wrist injury and residual effects (such as pain), they struggle to open door knobs with their injured hand. The solutions they had found in their daily life have been using their other (non-dominant) hand, and preferring doors with levers or push operation.

Interviewee 2

Brandeis student from Massachusetts with a history of shoulder injuries from various athletic pursuits. (Dodgeball, volleyball, quadball) Has had 2 surgeries to prevent future dislocations of the shoulder. They don’t find door knobs too inconvenient, but agreed that push doors and handles were definitely preferable options.

Interviewee 3

Brandeis rugby player who injured their wrist over time while playing. They have not been to a doctor, but notice their injury getting worse when falling/tackling and catching. They have door knobs in their dorm, and will often use their non-dominant hand (not injured) to open doors to avoid pain.
